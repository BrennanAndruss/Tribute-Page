Timeline

  • 1944 - Born in Clarksville, Texas
  • 1963 - Running for Lemoore High School, he won the 440-yard dash in the CIF California State Meet. He was Lemoore's "Most Valuable Athlete" for football, basketball, and track and field.
  • 1966 - Running for San Jose State University, he set the world record for the 200 meters and for the 220-yard dash. His team also won the NCAA Men's Outdoor Track and Field Championship.
  • 1967 - Drafted into the NFL by the Los Angeles Rams and signed in the AFL by the Cincinnati Bengals
  • 1967 - Won gold for the 200m and silver for the 100m at the FISU Wold University Games
  • 1968 - Running for Team USA, Smith won gold for the 200m at the 1968 Summer Olympics, with teammate Carlos earning the silver. At the medal award ceremony, both athletes stood barefoot and rose a black-gloved fist as a protest of the living conditions of minorities in the US. Both athletes were banned from the Olympic Village.
  • 1969 - Graduates from San Jose State University with a BA in Social Science
  • 1978 - Inducted into the US National Track and Field Hall of Fame
  • 1996 - Inducted into the California Black Sports Hall of Fame
  • 2005 - A statue showing Smith and Carlos on the medal stand is dedicated on the campus of San Jose State University
  • 2007 - Published his autobiography, Silent Gesture, which was nominated for a 2008 NAACP Image Award
  • 2022 - Published his second book, Victory. Stand! Raising My Fist for Justice, which won the YALSA Award for Excellence in Nonfiction and was a finalist for the 2022 National Book Award for Young People's Literature
